Cooperation and results

Dialogue that leads to action

The Hub is intended to structure problems, identify agreement and divergence, and turn market experience into practical proposals.

Expected results

What should the Hub produce?

Each topic should conclude with a structured understanding of the problem and a proposal for further action.

The Polish Hub is intended to produce concrete results that can be used in practice, not merely an exchange of opinions. Not every process must end in full agreement. A clear description of the conditions required for implementation, remaining differences and possible options for further action can also be a valuable output.

Recommendations

For public authorities, banks and other financial institutions.

Financial instruments

Proposals for new products or changes to existing solutions.

Standards

Data, documentation, reference values and project-assessment methods.

Simplification

Procedures proportionate to investment scale and lower transaction costs.

Risk allocation

Models of responsibility for investors, auditors, contractors, financiers and public institutions.

Pilots

Solutions that allow proposed mechanisms to be tested in practice.

Project pipelines

Action supporting the preparation and aggregation of investment-ready projects.

Future topics

Identification of problems requiring further work and new working groups.

Invitation

Who can participate?

The Polish Hub is open and relies on the knowledge and experience of market participants. KAPE invites public institutions, banks and other financial institutions, companies, local authorities, industry organisations, energy service companies, auditors and experts.

Participation should be working-oriented. The most important contribution is experience, data and perspectives that help describe a problem more accurately and assess the feasibility of proposed solutions.

Forms of involvement

  • participation in working groups and substantive discussion;
  • presentation of experience, needs and positions;
  • review of proposed solutions;
  • provision of data and practical examples;
  • suggestion of topics requiring joint analysis;
  • cooperation in designing and assessing pilots.
The Hub is intended to provide a space in which different perspectives can be confronted in open discussion and then translated into solutions that increase the scale and effectiveness of energy-efficiency financing in Poland.
02
Starting point

Market diagnosis

“Financing energy efficiency: systemic and institutional barriers”

One of the starting points for the Hub work programme is a KAPE publication based on in-depth interviews with representatives of banks, public institutions, operators of financial instruments, local authorities, companies and other market participants.

The study shows that barriers to energy-efficiency financing arise not only from a lack of capital, but also from misaligned procedures, information, assessment methods and risk allocation. The identified problems and recommended directions provide an important basis for selecting topics for the Hub and its working groups.
