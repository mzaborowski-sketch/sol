Work programme

Initial working groups

The first two topics combine the development of repayable and blended instruments with improvements to non-financial elements of the financing system.

01
Scope of work

Two connected areas of work

One group focuses on the design of financial instruments, while the other addresses the system conditions that determine their effectiveness.

The Polish Hub programme will be developed around specific problems identified by market participants. The first two groups address complementary issues: the design and distribution of financial instruments and the system conditions affecting project preparation, assessment and financeability.

Both groups will work from practical experience, data and analysis. The purpose is not to produce another general diagnosis, but to identify implementation conditions and prepare recommendations useful to public authorities and financial institutions.

I
Financial instruments

Development of repayable and blended instruments

The group will focus on experience with loans, credit products and instruments combining repayable finance with public support. Its starting point will be why existing solutions have not always achieved the expected scale and what conditions are needed to make them attractive to investors and financial institutions.

  • assessment of experience with existing instruments;
  • analysis of demand and real attractiveness to clients;
  • costs of preparing, distributing and maintaining bank offers;
  • alignment of credit procedures with public-support procedures;
  • the role of promotion, information, advice and available sales channels;
  • the benefit a blended instrument should provide compared with a traditional grant;
  • recommendations and possible pilot solutions.
“The investor goes through two processes: the grant process and the credit process. They use different assessment criteria and duplicate one another. This is a problem.”interview 38 | bank, “Financing energy efficiency: systemic and institutional barriers”, p. 57
Expected result: identification of conditions under which repayable and blended instruments can reach appropriate scale, providing a clear benefit to clients and a sound case for the implementation and distribution costs borne by financial institutions.
II
System elements

Improving the effectiveness of the energy-efficiency financing system

The second group will focus on non-financial elements that affect the cost, timing and financeability of investment. Its purpose is to preserve the credibility of information on energy effects while reducing unnecessary procedural and analytical burdens.

  • simplification and better proportionality of procedures to project type and scale;
  • better quality and practical use of project information;
  • distinguishing projects requiring full, simplified or standardised assessment;
  • reference values and standards for repeatable measures;
  • responsibility for declared and achieved energy effects;
  • the possible role of banks and other institutions in assessing project information;
  • monitoring results and using experience from completed investments.
“A systemic solution is to differentiate the functions of the audit and move away from the principle of ‘an audit always and everywhere’. Simple and repeatable modernisation measures do not require individual modelling and can be covered by standardised solutions.”authors’ analytical conclusions, “Financing energy efficiency: systemic and institutional barriers”, pp. 93–94
Possible result: initial proposals for standardised measures with assigned energy effects, uncertainty levels and documentation requirements, together with a better allocation of responsibility and risk.
01
Planned work cycle

Each topic has a starting point and a result

Groups continue until the problem has been exhausted rather than according to a fixed meeting calendar.

Start of work

Definition of the problem, starting point and questions requiring an answer.

Verification of diagnosis

Collection of market experience and stakeholder positions, and completion of evidence.

Analysis

Definition of boundary conditions, costs, risks and possible action options.

Consultation of solutions

Assessment from the perspectives of public institutions, finance and investors.

Recommendations

Preparation of results, implementation options and possible pilots.

After completing its work, a group may address another problem or be replaced by a new working group. This preserves a permanent Hub structure while allowing the programme to change with market needs.
