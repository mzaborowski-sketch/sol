Objectives, organisation and working method

Polish Hub

A permanent cooperation platform that turns market diagnosis into practical recommendations and solutions applicable in Poland.

01
Role of the Hub

A continuous work process, not a series of meetings

The Hub brings together market knowledge and focuses it on problems requiring joint analysis and coordinated action.

The Polish Energy Efficiency Financing Hub is intended to be a permanent cooperation platform for public administration, financial institutions, companies, local authorities, industry organisations and experts. Its activities should not be limited to a general exchange of views; they should lead to results useful to institutions designing, implementing and financing energy-efficiency action.

The Hub will focus on specific barriers for which a diagnosis can be prepared, stakeholder positions collected, implementation conditions defined and recommendations developed. Not every topic must end in full agreement: a clear description of differences and possible options for further action can also be a valuable result.

1

Increase private-capital participation

Create conditions in which private finance can support a greater share of investment.

2

Use public funds more effectively

Treat them as a means of mobilising additional capital, not as the sole source of support.

3

Simplify access to finance

Match requirements and procedures to the type, scale and risk of the investment.

4

Reduce risk and transaction costs

Use standardisation, better data and appropriate allocation of responsibility.

5

Develop instruments and support models

Products should reflect real investor needs and the capabilities of financial institutions.

6

Stimulate demand and project pipelines

Support the preparation of more investments ready for financing and aggregation.

02
Origins

Development of the national Hub

The creation of the Hub is the result of cooperation among institutions responsible for energy-efficiency policy and transition finance.

An important step was a request from NFOŚiGW to the Ministry of Climate and Environment to assess the case for establishing a national hub within the European Energy Efficiency Financing Coalition.

In March 2025, the Ministry's Department for District Heating Transition and Energy Efficiency considered the establishment of the Hub justified. It stressed the need to increase private-capital participation, improve financing conditions and support implementation of the EED and EPBD directives.

The Ministry also indicated that the coordinating role should be performed by an organisation active in the fields covered by both directives and experienced in cooperation with the relevant ministries. In this context, possible involvement of KAPE was identified.

04
Working method

How do the working groups operate?

A group is established to address a defined problem and continues until the topic has been exhausted and results prepared.

Starting point

A diagnosis of experience, barriers, stakeholder positions and questions requiring resolution.

Verification

Completing the diagnosis, gathering data and confronting different perspectives.

Implementation conditions

Identifying legal, organisational, financial and technical requirements.

Proposals

Developing action options, recommendations, standards or pilot solutions.

Feasibility assessment

Checking whether the proposals can be applied in practice and by whom.

After completing a topic, the group may take up another area or be replaced by a new group. This keeps the Hub structure permanent while allowing its programme to respond flexibly to changing market needs.
Secretariat

Role of KAPE S.A.

KAPE has worked in effective energy management since 1994. It combines experience in technical and economic advisory services, audits, national and international projects, and support for companies and local authorities.

As Secretariat, KAPE organises and coordinates the work, provides analytical support, assists the working groups and turns participant knowledge and experience into recommendations.

Condition for effectiveness

The effectiveness of the Hub depends on close cooperation with public administration, NFOŚiGW, banks and other financial institutions, companies, local authorities, industry organisations and experts.

KAPE is not intended to replace those actors. Its role is to create a space in which their perspectives can be structured and used to prepare solutions applicable in practice.
