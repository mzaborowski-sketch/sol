LIFE project and European cooperation

EEFINH — support for developing the Polish Hub

The project provides the organisational, expert and financial framework for Hub activities and exchange with partners in other EU countries.

Role of the project

The mandate and core tasks of the Hub derive from the European Energy Efficiency Financing Coalition. The LIFE project enables their practical implementation through stakeholder cooperation, working groups, analytical work and recommendations, and exchange with hubs in other countries.

EEFINH project

Activities are implemented under the “Energy Efficiency Financing National Hubs” project — EEFINH, LIFE24-CET-EEFINH, No. 101250182. The project started on 1 July 2025 and will run until 30 June 2029.

It is coordinated by the Italian agency ENEA, while KAPE is the partner responsible for developing and operating the national Hub in Poland.

European cooperation

The project includes partners from 14 EU countries. Its common structure enables systematic exchange of knowledge, experience and good practice while preserving each Hub’s autonomy in setting national priorities and organising its work.

Poland can draw on solutions developed elsewhere while contributing experience from its own financing system and support programmes.

Meaning for Poland

What does the project enable?

Project support is used not merely to organise meetings, but to build a permanent cooperation process and analytical base.

1

Organisation and coordination of the Hub

Maintaining a permanent structure, work programme, participant communication and documentation of results.

2

Working groups

Creating a space for in-depth work on specific problems and possible solutions.

3

Diagnosis, analysis and recommendations

Preparing materials that structure knowledge and support the assessment of possible action options.

4

Engagement of public authorities, finance and the market

Connecting institutions that influence the design, financing and implementation of investment.

5

Exchange with other hubs

Comparing experience, adapting good practice and presenting Polish solutions at European level.

6

Development of implementable solutions

Moving from diagnosis and discussion to recommendations, standards, options and pilots.

Meaning for KAPE and the market

Two-way exchange of experience

KAPE will contribute Polish experience to the European network of national hubs and use solutions and findings developed in other countries. The project makes it possible to adapt European approaches to Poland and present Polish experience at European level.
